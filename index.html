Exploring the Gender Pay Gap

In 2023, women only earned 81¢ per dollar

Historically, women have earned less than men in nearly every field, but the size of the gap has shifted over time for each field — and it isn’t the same everywhere.


In this report, we examine the gender pay gap across a range of occupations over the past decade.

Do women enter into lower-paying occupations?

Could the gender wage gap be due to women entering lower-paying fields?


To examine that, we looked at the proportion of male and female workers in each occupation, sorted by median pay — highlighting which fields lean heavily male, which lean heavily female, and which remain balanced.


It’s clear that some occupations are strongly gendered.
Fields like Healthcare Support and Healthcare Practitioners are predominantly held by women,
while Construction and Maintenance remain overwhelmingly male‑dominated.
Other categories, such as Legal and Business & Finance, hovered just above 50% female, challenging some of our assumptions.


This observation led us to consider:

Does the percentage of women in an occupation relate to how large or small the gender pay gap is within that field?

Are female‑dominated industries more equitable when it comes to compensation — or does the gap persist regardless of gender representation?

How has that gap changed in the past decade?
